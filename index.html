Life Timeline

  • 1890 Born in Torquay, England.
  • 1896 The little Agatha moves to France at the age of six with her family.
  • 1914 Archie and Agatha marry on Boxing Day.
  • 1917 The Mysterious Affair at Styles is completed but no one wishes to publish it! It is turned down by 6 publishers before finally being published three years later by The Bodley Head.
  • 1919 Agatha gives birth to Rosalind.
  • 1926 Agatha's beloved mother, Clarissa, passes away after suffering a bout of Bronchitis.
  • 1928 Agatha is granted a divorce from her husband, Archie, He will later marry the woman he was having an affair with.
  • 1930 Agatha collaborates with other crime writers, for a popular BBC radio programme called behind the Screen. Her first radio episode is called, Something is Missing and sadly no longer survives.
  • 1930 Much to the disapproval of her older sister Madge, Agatha marries Max. They choose to marry in Scotland.
  • 1940 Agatha begins writing the very last Poirot story. This will not be published for another 35 years.
  • 1941 Agatha begins her career as a playwright.
  • 1943 Agatha pens Absent in the Spring, a Mary Westmacott novel, in an exhausting three-day writing frenzy.
  • 1946 Agatha begins work on her fourth Mary Westmacott novel.
  • 1951 In time The Mousetrap will become Agatha's most famous play. it will win a Guinness World Record for the longest continuously running play in the world. You can still see it at St Martin's Theatre, London.
  • 1956 Agatha is awarded a CBE, by the Queen in the New Years Honours list. CBE stands for Commander of the British Empire.
  • 1965 15 years after she first started writing it, Agatha completes her autobiography. It will be published after her death.
  • 1976 Agatha dies at Winterbrook House aged 85.
